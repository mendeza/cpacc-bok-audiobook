CPACC Body of Knowledge read by a human

The audio files currently linked here are quick takes, not production quality. For this first draft no media player is provided, rather native browser support for the audio element is required. The recordings here so far are prototypes that serve a few purposes: to test my current audio production setup after a long period of disuse; to experiment with reading and recording nuances; to elicit feedback on same from a close group of beta testers; to serve as raw material for exploration of DAISY formatted DT Book encoding.

The audio files here begin with Content Outline Domain One A and skip the actual document's opening section that includes the Table of Contents, The Purpose of this Document, IAAP CPACC Exam Preparation Resources, About the CPACC Professional Certification, The CPACC Certification Content Outline Concepts At-A-Glance, and Additional CPACC Resources and Other IAAP Information.

In the recordings for Domain One A, the models of disability, I aurally indicated section headers and bullet lists. The next day I spoke with a legally blind person who informed me this would be superfluous in most circumstances, and certainly in the case of DT Book production. Accordingly, these aural indications were dropped in the next set of recordings, for Domain One B. Note that only five sections of Domain One B are included here.
